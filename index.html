About me

I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Science at the University of Liverpool (UK), zealous about interdisciplinary research between Machine Learning and Science. I am pursuing my studies under the supervision of Vladimir V. Gusev and in collaboration with the Leverhulme research centre for functional materials design. I received Master's degree in experimental particle physics from the University of Pisa (IT), where I conducted my thesis within the Mu2e experiment at the Fermi National Laboratory (US), under the supervision of Stefano Di Falco and Robert H. Bernstein.

What i'm doing

  • Spatio-temporal ML

    My primary area of research is the development of machine-learning protocols to analyze data that exhibit both a spatial and temporal structure.

  • Multivariate Time-series

    I study the interdependence between patterns in Multivariate Time-series datasets.

  • Weathering predictions

    I use full-history weathering data to predict long term performances in untested locations. Ultimately, the extraction of greater formulatory information can guide the design of safe and durable materials.

  • Other real-world applications

    My research is inspired by real-world problems, which include climatology, green-energy energy output, epidemiology, general healthcare and traffic predictions.
